Techniques

Even with the right tools and recipes, it’s technique that transforms your coffee. But, don’t worry, you don’t need professional training! These simple skills will help you brew smoother espresso, steam milk like a pro, and pour drinks that taste (and look) amazing.

Technique 1: Grinding

Why it matters: The grind size controls how your coffee tastes. Too coarse and your coffee will taste weak and sour; too fine and it can turn bitter.

How to do it right:

  • Use a burr grinder for consistent results.
  • Match grind size to brewing method (coarse for French press, fine for espresso).
  • Adjust gradually until your coffee tastes balanced.

Common mistake: Using one grind size for everything. Each brew method needs its own

Technique 2: Tamping (for Expresso)

Why it matters: Tamping compresses the coffee evenly so water flows through it at the right speed. Uneven tamping leads to watery or bitter shots.

How to do it right:

  • Fill portafilter with finely ground coffee.
  • Use a tamper to press firmly and evenly.
  • Keep the surface level.

Common mistake: Tamping unevenly, so the water will rush through weak spots.

Technique 4: Pouring & Latte Art Basics

Why it matters: The way you pour milk changes the flavor balance and presentation. Even a simple pour can make your drink café-like.

How to do it right:

  • Start with a slow pour into the espresso.
  • Tilt cup slightly for better control.
  • As you reach the top, pour more steadily to create foam patterns.

Common mistake: Pouring too fast and dumping all the milk in at once.

Technique 5: Extraction Time

Why it matters: The timing of espresso extraction controls strength, aroma, and taste.

How to do it right:

  • Aim for 25–30 seconds per shot.
  • If too fast: grind finer. If too slow: grind coarser.
  • Consistency is key.

Common mistake: Ignoring brew time and hoping for the best.
